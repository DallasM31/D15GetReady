For Emergencies Requiring Outside Assistance


Who Should Dial 911?

Anyone having an emergency which requires immediate respose because of:

  • A medical problem
  • A fire
  • A person's life or well being is threatened
  • Property could be destroyed
  • An illeagal activity is in progress

What Happens When You Call 911?

A well trained County 911 Operator will immediately asl you if you have a Fire, Police or Ambulance emergency. you should calmly explain which service you are in need of, after which you will be instantly connected with either a Police or Fire Department Dispatcher.

What Information Will The Dispatcher Need?

  • Your address
  • The closest intersecting road to your home
  • Your name
  • Your telephone number
  • A breif description of the emergency
  • If you are unable to speak, keep the phone line open. It will provide dispatchers a means of locating you

Remain Calm

It is important that you remain calm. carefully provide the needed information and follow all directions

Let The Dispatcher Tell You When To Hang Up

Do not hang up until the dispatcher has all the neccessary information. The Fire department has an Emergency Dispatcher on duty at all times who may be able to provide emergency medical self-help over the phone, while equipment is on the way to your home.

Help Us Find You

Each property in Salt Lake County has been assigned a street address. Display this number with large numerals on your mailbox or in front of your home so Polcie Officers or Fire Fighters will not be delayed in locating you.
